Power and PSUs: Selection Guide

To ensure the stability of your components, follow these instructions:

  • Wattage Calculation: Add up the TDP of your CPU and GPU, and add a 30% margin for transient spikes.
  • Certification: Prioritize 80 Plus Gold or higher power supplies to reduce energy loss as heat.
  • ATX 3.0 Standard: If using RTX 40 series cards, use power supplies with native 12VHPWR connector to avoid adapters.
  • Cables: Connect independent cables for each GPU port; avoid using "pigtail" (dual-head) cables on high-consumption hardware.

Cooling and Airflow Instructions

Optimize your build's temperatures with these critical steps:

  • Positive Pressure: Make sure you have more fans bringing air in (front/bottom) than exhausting (rear/top) to prevent dust entry.
  • AIO Mounting: The radiator must be above the pump. If mounted at the front, tubes should be at the bottom to avoid air bubbles.
  • Thermal Paste: Apply a pea-sized amount in the center; the heatsink pressure will do the rest. Always clean with 90%+ isopropyl alcohol.

Gaming Optimization and Software

Configuration to squeeze every FPS from your hardware:

  • Re-Size BAR: Enable it in BIOS and verify in NVIDIA/AMD Control Panel to improve CPU-GPU communication.
  • XMP/EXPO Profile: Enter BIOS and enable the memory profile; otherwise, your DDR5 RAM will run at base speed (4800MHz).
  • Game Mode: Disable "Core Isolation" in Windows 11 if you experience micro-stuttering in competitive games.

Warranty and RMA Protocol

Mandatory steps to process a successful return:

  • Visual Inspection: Before shipping, photograph the socket pins or PCIe slot to prove there's no prior physical damage.
  • Anti-Static Packaging: It's mandatory to ship GPUs and Motherboards in their original gray bag to prevent ESD damage during transport.
  • Backup: Erase your data from SSDs before shipping them. ComponentBox is not responsible for file loss during testing.
